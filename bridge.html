Cordillera Central, Dominican Republic
Where It Begins

Grown by Hands That Know the Land

In the highest peaks of the Dominican Republic, where the clouds settle between the ridges of the Cordillera Central, families have cultivated coffee for generations. They pick each cherry by hand, dry the beans under the Caribbean sun, and roast in small batches — the way it has always been done.

This is not commodity coffee. This is a family's legacy, carried in every bag that leaves the mountain.

3,000 miles. One family.

Where It Arrives

A Piece of Home on the Shelf

In a small tienda on the west side of Jacksonville, between the Bustelo and the Pilón, you'll find a bag of Manabao. The owner stocks it because he knows what it means — not just coffee, but a connection to a place that shaped you.

For the Dominican families in this city, finding Manabao on the shelf is like hearing a familiar voice in a crowded room.
